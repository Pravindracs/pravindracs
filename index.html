Research

Intelligent Fault Detection in WSNs

Wireless Sensor Networks (WSNs) are widely used in healthcare, smart cities, environmental monitoring, and industrial automation, but their performance is highly affected by faults such as sensor malfunctions, node failures, communication errors, and environmental interference. Traditional threshold-based fault detection methods lack adaptability and scalability. Intelligent fault detection integrates artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) techniques to autonomously identify, classify, and correct faults in real time. By leveraging supervised and unsupervised learning, anomaly detection, and deep learning approaches, these systems enhance accuracy, robustness, and energy efficiency. With the adoption of edge computing, federated learning, and digital twin technologies, intelligent fault detection enables WSNs to evolve into reliable, self-healing, and adaptive sensor networks, ensuring trustworthy data acquisition in critical applications.

Edge Computing Based Real-Time Smart Fault Detection

The rapid growth of the Internet of Things (IoT) and Wireless Sensor Networks (WSNs) has accelerated the demand for real-time and intelligent fault detection mechanisms. Traditional cloud-based fault detection methods often suffer from high latency, bandwidth limitations, and energy inefficiency. Edge computing addresses these challenges by enabling computation close to the data source, thus reducing response time and enhancing scalability. Integrating edge intelligence with machine learning (ML) allows for real-time smart fault detection, where faults such as sensor malfunctions, communication errors, and environmental interferences are autonomously identified and corrected. This approach ensures low-latency, energy-efficient, and adaptive operation in critical domains like healthcare, industrial IoT, smart cities, and environmental monitoring.
